About

The vault takes stablecoin deposits and allocates them into tokenized exposure to Cain's LiquidStone II fund, which invests in FX-hedged, short-dated Brazilian credit card receivables for yield generation and Superstate's USCC market-neutral strategy for liquidity management.

Cash flows generated by the underlying assets are automatically reinvested into the vault, with returns reflected directly in the aLupal token price rather than distributed — providing passive exposure to diversified, real-world credit income.

Risk FAQ

What are the primary risks associated with this vault?
The primary risks include credit risk from the underlying Brazilian credit card receivables, FX volatility between BRL and USD (though hedged), and liquidity risk associated with the redemption window. Market-neutral strategies in Superstate's USCC also carry specific smart contract and operational risks.
How is the FX hedging managed?
FX hedging is managed at the fund level using rolling forward contracts to minimize exposure to BRL fluctuations, ensuring the yields are delivered in USD terms for vault participants.
Who provides the security audits for the vault?
The vault's smart contracts have undergone rigorous audits by leading firms including OpenZeppelin and Trail of Bits. Continuous monitoring is provided by Forta.
